In an (idealized) billiard-ball system, angular uncertainty goes up exponentially with collisions. So information about individual ball positions are lost except information about energy, because energy is conserved. This analogy tells us that information relevant far away into the future is an important property for an [[Abstraction|abstraction]].

This is relevant to the [[Telephone theorem|telephone theorem]].
