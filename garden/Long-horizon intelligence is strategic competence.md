Strategic competence is “intelligence” on long-horizon low-feedback tasks

Things can have really competent on feedback-loop-rich domains but not on domains with slow/noisy/inefficient feedback loops. Examples:
- Terence Tao is incredibly smart but solves math instead of [[AI safety|AI safety]] and stuff
- Some billionaires spend money on random shit
- Current ([[2026-03-08]]) frontier LLMs are even worse, they are good at coding/mathematics but very bad at philosophy (bad example)

> With very few exceptions, billionaires spend or give away their money haphazardly, philosophers don't bother to think about long term implications of AI on philosophy production (positive or negative), Terence Tao spends his time wireheading on abstract math instead of doing anything remotely like instrumental convergence.

\- Wei Dai ([[info, Wei Dai's Shortform — LessWrong, long horizon thinking and whatever|source]])
